The Problem
"Vendors negotiate thousands of contracts a year. You negotiate a few. That gap is costing you, quietly, consistently, and at scale."

It's not a competence problem. The CFO is sharp. The ops team is good. But vendor negotiation is a discipline, built through repetition, institutional knowledge, and pattern recognition most businesses never accumulate.

Traditional procurement was supposed to fix this. Instead it became a process function: slow, compliance-focused, measured on the wrong things, and structurally misaligned with the speed and commercial ambition of the business it was supposed to serve.

The function failed the mission. The mission still matters.

What's Actually Happening
Most businesses are overpaying vendors, carrying supplier relationships they should have cut years ago, and signing contracts that hand leverage to the other side. Nobody flagged it because nobody wanted the awkward conversation.
What Traditional Procurement Did About It
Built process. Created compliance requirements. Added headcount. Produced reports. Rarely changed the commercial outcome in any meaningful way.
What Actually Moves the Number
Commercial intelligence. Knowing what you should pay, what the vendor's real position is, where your leverage is, and how to use it. That's not a department. It's a discipline. And it's buildable.
A Different Model

The procurement era is over.
The commercial era is here.

Commercial orchestration isn't procurement with a new name. It's a fundamentally different way of thinking about every dollar a business spends: who controls it, how it's deployed, and what it's supposed to return.

How We Work

Where do you need to go?

Three different starting points. One destination: a commercial function that actually performs. The right path depends on where you are today.

Build
Commercial Architecture from Zero
You don't have a procurement function. Or what you have is a person with a job title and no real system. Either way, every vendor deal is being negotiated from scratch, without leverage, without institutional knowledge, and without a playbook.
  • Full spend landscape diagnostic: where the money is actually going
  • Vendor portfolio rationalization: who stays, who goes, who gets renegotiated
  • Commercial playbook built around your spend profile and risk posture
  • Custom negotiation intelligence tool embedded with your company's stance
  • Execution roadmap your team can run without us
For organizations building commercial capability for the first time, or rebuilding it right.
Scale
From Tactical to Strategic
You have people doing procurement work. They're capable. But the function is still operating tactically: reactive, process-heavy, measured on savings that don't reflect real commercial impact. It needs to grow up without growing bigger.
  • Commercial maturity assessment: honest read on where the gaps are
  • Operating model redesign built for speed and strategic alignment
  • KPI framework that measures commercial impact, not process compliance
  • Capability building: your team learns to think and operate like seasoned commercial leaders
  • Tooling and intelligence layer that scales without adding headcount
For organizations with a functioning procurement team that's not operating at its ceiling.
Fix
Reset a Broken Function
The function exists. It's staffed. It has processes. And it still isn't delivering. Slow sourcing cycles, vendor relationships nobody manages, contracts that keep auto-renewing, savings that exist only on a slide. Something structural is wrong.
  • Honest audit of what's working, what isn't, and why
  • Supplier portfolio rationalization: cut what shouldn't exist
  • Process redesign focused on speed, accountability, and commercial outcomes
  • Organizational realignment: right people, right roles, right incentives
  • Commercial KPIs that replace vanity metrics with real impact measurement
For organizations with an existing function that's slow, bloated, or structurally misaligned with the business.
